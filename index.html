Using Monte Carlo Method to Calculate RNG-Based Game

Let's say you are playing an RNG game. There's a rare loot from a boss which only drop 1% each time you kill him. So you have to grind that.

The question is: if you're grinding 100 times , what are the odds that you'll get at least 1 loot drop ?

One solution is to use complex statistical method to solve it. But since we have the power of computational system in our hands, why not use it?

We're going to simulate, let's say 10000 people who are going to grind that boss. Each person are going kill 100 bosses. We're going to see the loot drop from these people.
